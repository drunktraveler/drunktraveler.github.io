Zanarkand and Ruins

  • Upon starting the game, you get to choose between the Standard and Expert Sphere grid. If you want to max out your stats both are fine, but reaching 99.999HP and 9.999MP would require the Standard grid. The biggest advantage of the Expert grid is the early access to the Steal and Use abilities.
  • Tell the girl wearing a pink shirt you're getting her spare tickets (talk to her 3 times for it) and get Potion (x2) from her
  • Meet Auron and get the Longsword
  • Optional: Get Tidus to half HP and kill Auron, press triangle until you learn Daredevil (170 turns) and Loner (60 turns), in the next battle kill the sinspawn appearing infinitely from the back to receive Slayer (100 kills)
  • Kill Esum and continue to the Submerged Ruins
  • Get Potion (x2) from a chest on the left
  • Optional: Load your Al-Bhed progress from a different save
  • Get 200 Gil from a chest to the right
  • Take a left on the bridge and grab a Hi-Potion
  • Kill 2 of the Sahagins and wait for Tidus to escape
  • Get some flint from the room next to the save-sphere
  • Pick up the X-Potion in the northwest of the main room, next to a locked door
  • Grab an Ether left after entering the northern entrance (try walking down for the camera to show the chest)
  • Follow the path for Withered Bouquet and a Hi-Potion at the end
  • Start a fire, steal a few Grenades from Krik and kill it
  • After waking up on the ship, pick up Al Bhed Primer Vol. I next to the crane
  • Get Potion (x3) from the guy in the northwest
  • Optional: After diving and climbing onto the ship again, the same guy will give you another Potion (x3). You can repeat this process until you have 99 Potions (33 trips)
  • Optional: Set Tidus Overdrive Mode to Loner and kill Rikku in a random encounter. Keep blocking until Tidus Overdrive is available and use it 10/30/80 times to learn Slaughter/Energy Rain/Blitz Ace (est. 1h)
  • Head into the ruins and kill Tros

Besaid

  • Head into an alcove to the southeast to pick up the Moon Crest
  • West of the beach is a chest with Antidote (x2)
  • Talk to all Aurochs and Wakka to get 200 Gil, Potion (x3), Potion (x2) and a Hi-Potion

  • Pick up the Al Bhed Primer Vol. II inside the blue tent and save your game
  • Start the Cloister of Trials
  • Don't forget to solve the puzzle with the Destruction Sphere - leading to a secret room with the Rod of Wisdom
  • After obtaining Valefor, Wakka will give you the Brotherhood
  • Buy what you need from the item shop and talk to the girl with the dog until she tells you her dog found something. Go into the third eastern house and obtain Valefors' Overdrive Energy Blast
  • Beat Kimahri and receive a random piece of equipment
  • Optional: Return to Besaid and buy equipment
  • Talk to everyone at the beach to obtain 400 Gil, a Remedy, an Ether, a Seeker's Ring and Phoenix Down (x3)

Perfect Sphere Master

  • While it is completely possible to focus only on this achievement, this section also prepares for all other end-game achievements. This entails farming gil, all kinds of Spheres, Celestial Weapons, creation of custom weapons and armor and completing Monster Arena quests
  • Start off by capturing every monster in Spira 5 times - be careful Inside Sin and in The Omega Ruins
  • Obtain the Celestial Weapons for your party. Breaking the damage limit will make it much easier to farm Monster Creations later on
  • Customize a weapon for your preferred three party members with Overdrive -> AP (This will cost you 30 Doors of Tomorrow, but you will gain 99 for your monster captures)
  • Customize armor for your three members with Auto-Haste and Auto-Phoenix. Customize one Ribbon (so you don't waste potential Dark Matter drops later on). You can buy empty armor with 4 slots from Wattz in Macalania. You will be missing 141 Chocobo Wings, bribing a Machea 5 times for 180.000 gil each (900.000 gil) will reward you 150 Chocobo Wings
  • Fight Don Tonberry. Have the member with the most monster kills (To figure out who has most kills: Don Tonberry automatically counters with "Karma", an attack that deals monsterkills*100 damage) attack while the others block. The attacker should have Overdrive Mode "Stoic", the other two should have Overdrive Mode "Comrade"
  • You will frequently run out of basic Spheres while leveling. To solve this issue, kill Kottos after hitting him with a Distill Power/Mana/Ability/Speed. He will drop 20 (40 for an Overkill) of that sphere
  • You might also still need Lv. 1/2/3/4 Key Spheres
  • To activate the existing luck nodes, you need Fortune Spheres. Those are a little harder to farm, kill/overkill Earth Eater for 1/2 Spheres
    • Lv. 1 Key Sphere: Bribe Imps in the Cavern of Stolen Fayth with 17.600 gil for 4 Keys
    • Lv. 2 Key Sphere: Bribe Behemoths in Mt. Gagazet with 460.000 gil for 30 Keys
    • Lv. 3 Key Sphere: Bribe Demonoliths in Sin/Omega Ruins with 900.000 gil for 40 Keys
    • Lv. 4 Key Sphere: Bribe Chime Brains in Calm Lands with 196.000 gil for 2 Keys
  • Should you require gil at any point, you can equip Rikku's Celestial Weapon and kill Mimics in the Omega Ruins for 100.000 gil each. Alternatively, farming One-Eye and selling his weapon/armor drops is a good option. Or, you could buy a bunch of armor with one empty slot and customize Auto-Regen on it. This requires 80 Healing Springs, but you get 20 (40) from each Kottos kill - each piece of armor sells for 60.000 gil
  • Once you can comfortably defeat One-Eye, swap to your Celestial Weapons and farm it for weapons with 2 empty slots and Triple AP in the third slot for three of your party members. Customize Overdrive -> AP onto those weapons. Customize Triple Overdrive onto those weapons.
  • Customize Auto-Protect onto your armor. You will be missing 141, get those from killing Fafnir 8 (overkill 4) times, he drops 20 (40) on death. He might drop Dark Matter instead, just continue killing him in that case
  • Continue farming Don Tonberry with those party members until you can comfortably defeat Ultima Buster. Farm him for weapons that already have Triple AP, Triple Overdrive and Overdrive -> AP
    • Alternatively, instead of farming Ultima Buster, you can also continue to simply farm One-Eye for armors with Triple AP and two empty slots, then customize that gear for your purposes. You should already have 3 weapons with all three abilities, so only 4 more are required
    • Customizing Overdrive -> AP should be possible for 3 of your members, you will be missing one Door to Tomorrow. Steal one from the Bomb King
    • Customizing Triple Overdrive requires 30 Winning Formulas each. You should have 9 left, meaning you need to farm 111 more. Bribing Sand Worms in Bikanel for 900.000 gil will net you 15 each, so 8 bribes/7.200.000 gil will get you there
  • Optional: Now would also be a good time to finish customizing your armors for all party members. You should currently have 3 armors with Auto-Haste/Auto-Phoenix/Auto-Protect and one should have Ribbon as its final ability. Only Auto-Haste is a true requirement, you can get by without anything else
    • Auto-Haste: Chocobo Wings (x80) - Bribe Machea for 180.000 gil (for 30 Chocobo Wings) - meaning you would need to bribe 11 Macheas for 1.980.000 gil
    • Auto-Phoenix: Mega-Phoenix (x20) - Bribe Ghosts (Cavern of Stolen Fayth) for 99.999 gil (20 Mega Phoenixes) - meaning you would need to bribe 4 Ghosts for 399.996 gil
    • Ribbon: Dark Matter (x99) - You are given 99 for capturing every monster 5 times. The missing 594 can be farmed from One-Eye and Fenrir, who you have to farm regardless for Triple AP weapons and Agility Spheres. Should you already have the required weapons and spheres AND are strong enough to kill Dark Yojimbo, you can instead kill him 4 times (not 5!), leave the cave, repeat
    • Alternative Ribbon: You can farm Dark Yojimbo (kill him 4 times, leave the cave, repeat) for Ribbon armor. Customize everything else onto it afterwards (Meaning you'd have to defeat 4 (2) Fafnirs, bribe Machea twice (180.000 gil each) and a Ghost once (99.999 gil))
    • Auto-Protect: Light Curtain (x80) - Defeat Fafnir, he drops 20 (40 if overkilled) upon death, so you'd need to defeat him 16 (8) times
  • At this point, maxing out the stats is the goal. For this, we will remove as few nodes as possible and ignore getting to 99.999 HP and 9.999 MP to reduce farming as much as possible
  • Check out the stat maxing guide if your Sphere Grid differs from what is described here (https://gamefaqs.gamespot.com/ps2/197344-final-fantasy-x/faqs/46942) or if you want to max out HP and MP too
  • Farm all required Spheres, they are sorted in order of importance
    • Strength Sphere (x20): Defeat Juggernaut
    • Defense Sphere (x30): Defeat Tanket
    • Magic Defense Sphere (x32): Defeat One-Eye
    • Magic Sphere (x27): Defeat Jumbo Flan
    • Luck Sphere (x55): Defeat Greater Sphere
    • Fortune Sphere Sphere (x448): Defeat Earth Eater (448 Spheres because apart from the 55 you place, you also need to activate the 9 that exist. Alternatively, clear out the 4 Luck Spheres that are +1 and 2 that are +2, and replace them with 2 +4's -> meaning you need 6 Clear Spheres and 2 Luck Spheres more, but 28 Fortune Spheres less. There will be one +2 Luck Sphere left, but replacing it would be pointless
    • You will notice that there are no Evasion and Accuracy Spheres mentioned. Luck replaces those stats almost completely. If you will want to max them, kill Hornet for Accuracy and Pteryx for Evasion. Agility is also useless above 170 if you have Auto-Haste, and the Sphere Grid already offers 174 Agility. Should you decide to max Agility regardless, farm Fenrir for it.
    • You will have 80 free nodes left. If you wish to max your other stats, you would need 20 Agility Spheres, 32 Evasion Spheres and 46 Accuracy Spheres. So at that point, you can only choose 2/3 and then start using Clear Spheres and replacing +1 and +2 fields with +4s.
    • Achieving 255 in EVERY stat and reaching both 99.999 HP and 9.999 MP would require 859 nodes. We can only manipulate 775, so some form of compromise is required. You can defeat Ironclad for HP and Vidatu for MP Sheres

Baaj Temple

  • You started the game in this area. Before coming here, make sure you found the Destruction Sphere in the temples of Besaid, Kilika, Djose, Macalania, Bevelle and Zanarkand
  • Defeat Geosgaeno
  • In the room with the statues, pick up a Mega Phoenix and a Megalixir in the southeastern and southwestern corners
  • Obtain Anima

Remiem Temple

  • The part outside the temple can be found here
  • Make sure you have Valefor, Ifrit, Ixion, Shiva, Bahamut, Yojimbo and Anima before arriving here
  • Challenge Belgemine, defeat all her Aeons and obtain the rewards
    • Valefor: Lightning Gem (x4) or the Summoner's Soul if you missed it, Power Sphere (x4) thereafter
    • Ifrit: X-Potion (x30) or the Aeon's Soul if you missed it, Mana Sphere (x5) thereafter
    • Ixion: Chocobo Feater (x10), Power Sphere (x8) thereafter
    • Shiva: Mega-Potion (x60), Star Curtain (x6) thereafter
    • Bahamut: Flower Scepter, Mana Sphere (x8) thereafter
    • Yojimbo: Shadow Gem (x8), Power Sphere (x10) thereafter
    • Anima: Stamina Spring (x60), Mana Sphere (x10) thereafter
    • Magus Sisters: Shining Gem (x40), Power Sphere (x12) thereafter
  • Send Belgemine for the Moon Sigil
  • Obtain the Blossom Crown at the Monster Arena by capturing every fiend at Mt. Gagazet and its cave
  • Obtain the Magus Sisters

The Omega Ruins

  • Enter the hardest dungeon in the game (The Omega Ruins) by interacting with the map on the airship at the coordinates X: 71~73, Y: 35~36
  • Randomized chests explanation
  • Head north, then head east at the intersection for some random treasures
  • Go west at the start for more random chests
  • From there, head northwest, then west at the fork for more random chests
  • Back at the fork, head east for even more random chests and the Al Bhed Primer Vol. XXVI at the northeast side of the clearing
  • Head northwest along the the incline and touch the glyph. Back at the clearing, so south and to the top of the ramp, touch that glyph too and receive a Teleport Sphere
  • From the clearing, head east and defeat Ultima Weapon
  • In the area with the winding spiral, head west for a Friend Sphere
  • Head east and defeat Omega Weapon
  • Go into the area where you defeated Omega Weapon for a Magic Sphere

The Dark Aeons

  • The Aeons appear after you obtain your airship and are mostly beatable if you're able to beat the Unique Creations in the Monster Arena
  • Defeat Dark Valefor in front of the village of Besaid
  • Defeat Dark Ifrit in the final area of the Sanubia Desert
  • Defeat Dark Ixion in the Thunder Plains near a lightning rod
  • Defeat Dark Shiva at the Macalania Temple
  • Defeat Dark Bahamut in Zanarkand where you fought Yunalesca
  • Defeat Dark Yojimbo five times, started by going to the end of the cave where you obtain him
  • Defeat Dark Anima inside the cave on top of Mt. Gagazet - play the Blitzball minigame again to start the fight
  • Defeat Dark Magus Sisters at Mushroom Rock. If you run away before the fight starts long enough, you can fight them one by one
  • Defeat Penance, the hardest boss in the game, available as a destination on the airship after you beat all other Dark Aeons

  • Tidus - Swordplay: Stop a moving cursor when it's in the middle of the gauge

    • Aurochs Reels: Obtained from a Blitzball tournament and after Wakka fought at least 450 battles, same as Element Reels, but with an Aurochs emblem - line those up for an attack to all enemies with 112% power

  • Lulu - Fury: Rotate your Analog Stick repeatedly to perform the selected spell multiple times at around 42% of its original power

    • Bad Breath: Obtained from Malboro/Great Malboro, inflict poison, darkness, silence, sleep on all enemies

Blitzball Sidequest

  • Optional: Create your team. You can use the Auroch's team for everything or you can make your life easier by collecting better players: a great and easily accessible team for this is Tidus (FW), Wedge (FW), Brother (MF), Zalitz (DF), Ropp (DF), Miyu/Jumal (GK). This team is incredibly strong at low levels and includes only free agents and scales decently well - it is slightly outclassed at extremely high levels though. For the purpose of obtaining Wakka's Reels and Celestial Weapon this is good enough
  • If your goal is only to obtain Wakka's Reels and his Celestial Weapon, sign the players above for 3 games only
  • If you are willing to reset data to get your rewards as quick as possible, the fastest way to win games is to score a goal with Tidus Jecht Shot and then use Brother to hide behind your own goalkeeper - most of the time, the enemies wont do anything and you can afk until you win
  • If you don't want to reset data, you can still open/close the tournament window to refresh rewards there. For leagues, save before the last match and reload the game if the new league has an unwanted reward
  • Win 450 battles with Wakka before you start (e.g. capture a few monsters for the Monster Arena)
  • Start your first league and play a few matches until you can participate in a tournament. If the reward is not Attack Reels, revisit the Blitzball screen a few times for the rewards to be rerolled
  • After winning the tournament, reset data until Status Reels shows up as a league reward and hire your team members for 13 games
  • After winning the league, open and close the tournament window until Aurochs Reels show up as a reward
  • After winning that tournament too, reset data again until the Jupiter Sigil shows up as a league reward and recruit your team again for another 10 games
  • Win the league, talk to the barkeeper in Luca and obtain World Champion for Wakka (this can be done anytime after winning the first tournament)

FAQ

The foundation of this checklist was based on Stephen McNabb's Dark Souls 2 Cheat Sheet and ZKjellberg's Dark Souls 3 Cheat Sheet.

The content is based on jegged's Walkthrough, FFX FAQ/Walkthrough (PS3) by KeyBlade999 and Dansg08 video series.

I have feedback, how can I contribute?

You can visit the GitHub repository and report Issues or create a fork and submit a Pull Request.

Is this a guide?

Nope, not really. This is a set of checklists and information that you can use while playing FFX to make sure you don't miss an item or boss.

Do I need to follow the playthrough in this exact order?

You can revisit most areas later on but some items are completely missable. This guide highlights items as they become available and that are missable (WIP), so feel free to follow however you please. You can always check things off in a different order.

Can I use this for multiple save files?

Yup, use the profile selector and buttons in the options tab at the top of the page to setup multiple profiles.

How does the checklist status get saved?

The checklist is saved to your browser's local storage. Be careful when clearing your browser's cache as it will also destroy your saved progress.
